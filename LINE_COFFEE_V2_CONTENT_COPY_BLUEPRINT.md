# 1. Brand Voice

## 1.1 Main Voice

Line Coffee should sound:

* Premium
* Warm
* Confident
* Simple
* Trustworthy
* Coffee-focused
* Friendly but not cheap
* Elegant but not complicated

The brand must not sound like:

* A cheap supermarket offer
* A generic coffee store
* A childish flavored-drinks brand
* A foreign brand pretending to be local
* A technical coffee encyclopedia only
* A loud sales page

---

## 1.2 Arabic Tone

Arabic copy should be:

```text
مصري راقي
واضح
دافي
سهل
مش مبالغ
مش عامي زيادة
مش فصحى تقيلة
```

Use Arabic that feels natural for customers in Egypt.

Good Arabic tone:

```text
قهوة معمولة بمزاج، من توليفات مختارة بعناية.
```

Avoid:

```text
أفضل وأقوى وأرخص قهوة في مصر!!!
```

Avoid overusing:

```text
جامد جدًا
تحفة
أقوى حاجة
عرض نار
```

Unless used in social media, not main website.

---

## 1.3 English Tone

English copy should be:

```text
Premium
Minimal
Warm
Clear
Editorial
Coffee lifestyle
```

Good English tone:

```text
Crafted blends, warm aromas, and coffee made for your daily ritual.
```

Avoid:

```text
The best coffee ever at the cheapest prices!
```

---

# 2. Bilingual Content Rules

## 2.1 Arabic and English Are Both First-Class

Arabic is not a secondary translation.

English is not a word-for-word copy of Arabic.

Each language should feel natural.

---

## 2.2 Copy Structure

Every public page should support:

```text
Arabic title
English title
Arabic subtitle
English subtitle
Arabic description
English description
Arabic SEO title
English SEO title
Arabic SEO description
English SEO description
```

---

## 2.3 Translation Rule

Do not translate literally.

Translate meaning and mood.

Example:

Arabic:

```text
قهوة تفهم مزاجك.
```

English:

```text
Coffee crafted around your taste.
```

Not:

```text
Coffee that understands your mood.
```

---

## 2.4 Mixed Language Rule

Avoid ugly mixed text.

Bad:

```text
اعمل Customize للـ Blend بتاعك
```

Better:

```text
صمّم توليفتك بنفسك
```

or:

```text
Create your own blend
```

